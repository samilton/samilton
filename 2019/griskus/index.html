Pat Griskus Olympic

Date: June 15, 2019

Times

Swim: 0:29:18

Total: 2:29:49

Results

2⁄28 Age Group | 17⁄203 overall

Thoughts

Overall

This race replaced the Rev3 Quassy Olympic on my race calendar and boy was it a fun race. The energy amongst the athletes was more friendly than any other race I’d ever done. The organizers, volunteers, and athletes were all on the same page and it showed. Thanks for a great race and I can wait to return next year.

Swim

The swim was definitely longer than the typical Olympic course. Of course, I had trouble sighting as it was the first time I had ever done a diamond shaped swim and it was difficult making the turn at the top of the diamond. Needless to say, I swam a respectable pace at 1:30⁄100 over 2000 yards. I felt good on the swim but not as good as I had felt the previous week at the Harvest triathlon. I have a feeling that was due to the trouble sighting that I also seem to have on this particular lake. Despite the difficulty, I was happy with the effort.

T1

There was a longish run from the swim exit to the transition area but I was able to jog it and put in a reasonable 2:46 pace. I stumbled a bit with the wet suit and I also choose not to do a flying mount due to the quality of the tarmac in the transition area (it was bad).

Bike

The bike went much better than last week and I felt comfortable almost immediately. I have a feeling that this was partly due to some familiriaty with the beginning of the course. I found my rhythm and started cranking when I was immediately met with a climb that felt like it lasted forever. By my estimation we were climbing from mile 8 all the way through to 12.5. It then turned into a bit of a rolling course. This allowed me to settle back in and enjoy the remaining 12 miles of the race. Overall, I was happy with my effort and while my power output was a little low, I felt that this was a much more solid ride than the previous week. I was able to focus more on nutrition/hydration which soon be obvious on the run. The only big note I have on the bike is that I need to work on my descending skills. I definitely gave time and places due to a bit of over cautiousness when descending.

T2

T2 went the best it could have gone. My transition spot was well laid out and I was in and out in 1:37 seconds. Which wasn’t my fastest but definitely not my slowest T2.

Run

The run was brilliant. The course has a reputation of being difficult due to the amount of climbing but I felt great and was able to really push the pace. I started out a hot on the downhill out of transition and pushed a 7:06 first mile. The first 1.5 miles out to the first turn around went well and I felt great the whole way. A big part of this was having a chance to fall in with another athlete that was running at my pace. We ended up finishing the run together. On the way back the pace slowed a bit due to the climb but we were able to still turn out a sub 7:30/mile. Then it was a rinse and repeat on the second loop with almost identical results. Overall, while I was gassed at the end this was easily the best olympic run I have ever completed.

Final Thoughts

It was a dream to come across the finish line and see the clock under 02:30:00 hours. Just the previous week Jamie and I had joked that it would be a challenge to find the 6 minutes needed to make that possible. Fortunately, despite a longer than expected swim I was able to push the pace on the bike and then really push it on the run. This allowed for what once seemed out of reach to be a reality.

On to USAT Nationals in Cleveland!
